How the
fund works.

Ten percent of every KPT-UNDERGROUND transaction is held in a quarterly fund. At the close of each quarter, the fund is distributed to underground artists across the scenes the brand operates within — hardcore, gabber, hardtek, speedcore, techno, drum and bass, ambient — selected by people with genuine scene knowledge.

There is no threshold to meet, no genre requirement, no minimum follower count. The only criterion is making music that the scene recognises as honest — music that exists because it had to, not because a booking agency requested it.

IN THEIR OWN WORDS.

On making music outside the circuit

"What does it mean to you to operate completely outside the booking agency system?"

It means the music doesn't have to be anything other than what it is. No one is asking me to make it more accessible, more bookable, more radio-friendly. The only accountability is to the people in the room when it plays. That's an enormous freedom and an enormous responsibility simultaneously. The freedom means every decision is mine. The responsibility means there's no infrastructure to blame when it's wrong.

On the relationship between the music and the scene

"Do you think the underground has a responsibility to stay underground?"

I think it has a responsibility to stay honest. Underground is a means, not an end. If the music is honest and the environment it plays in is honest, the rest takes care of itself. What I'm suspicious of is the aesthetic of underground without the substance of it — the visual language of resistance deployed in service of the same commercial mechanisms it's supposed to be resisting.

On the fund

"What does direct artist support from a clothing brand mean in practice?"

It means I could afford the studio time for a release I'd been deferring for two years. That's it. That's the entire practical impact. No strings. No branding requirement. No expectation of acknowledgment. It arrived and it removed a material obstacle. That matters more than any artist partnership programme I've ever been approached about.
